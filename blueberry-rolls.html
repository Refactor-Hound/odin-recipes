Blueberry Rolls

Soft, pillowy rolls swirled with a sweet blueberry filling and drizzled with a tangy lemon glaze. These make a stunning breakfast or brunch treat that comes together with simple pantry ingredients.

Recipe Details

  • Prep Time: 30 minutes
  • Rise Time: 1 hour 30 minutes
  • Cook Time: 25 minutes
  • Total Time: 2 hours 25 minutes
  • Servings: 12 rolls
  • Calories: 310 per roll

Ingredients

Dough

  • 3 1/2 cups (440 g) all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons (1 packet) instant yeast
  • 1/4 cup (50 g) granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup (180 ml) whole milk, warmed to 110°F
  • 1/4 cup (60 g) unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Blueberry Filling

  • 2 cups (300 g) fresh or frozen blueberries
  • 1/3 cup (65 g) granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened (for spreading)

Lemon Glaze

  • 1 cup (120 g) powdered sugar, sifted
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1–2 tablespoons milk (to thin, as needed)

Instructions

  1. Make the dough. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, yeast, sugar, and salt. Add the warm milk, melted butter, eggs, and vanilla. Stir until a shaggy dough forms, then knead on a lightly floured surface for 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Alternatively, use a stand mixer with a dough hook on medium speed for 6 minutes.
  2. First rise. Place the dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover with plastic wrap or a clean towel, and let rise in a warm place for 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
  3. Make the blueberry filling. In a small saucepan over medium heat, combine blueberries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon zest, and lemon juice. Cook, stirring frequently, for 5–7 minutes until thickened. Remove from heat and let cool completely.
  4. Roll out the dough. Punch down the risen dough and turn it out onto a lightly floured surface. Roll into a rectangle approximately 16 x 12 inches.
  5. Add the filling. Spread the softened butter evenly over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border along one long edge. Spoon and spread the cooled blueberry filling over the butter.
  6. Roll and slice. Starting from the long edge with filling, tightly roll the dough into a log. Pinch the seam to seal. Using a sharp knife or unflavored dental floss, cut into 12 equal rolls.
  7. Second rise. Arrange rolls cut-side up in a greased 9 x 13-inch baking dish. Cover and let rise for 30 minutes, until puffed.
  8. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  9. Bake for 23–25 minutes, or until the tops are lightly golden. Do not overbake.
  10. Make the glaze. Whisk together powdered sugar, lemon juice, lemon zest, and milk until smooth and pourable.
  11. Glaze and serve. Drizzle the lemon glaze over the warm rolls. Serve immediately.

Notes & Tips

  • Frozen blueberries: Use straight from frozen; no need to thaw. Cook the filling a couple of minutes longer.
  • Cool the filling: Make sure the blueberry filling is fully cooled before spreading, or it will melt the butter and make the dough soggy.
  • Make ahead: Assemble the rolls the night before, cover tightly, and refrigerate. In the morning, let them come to room temperature for 30–45 minutes before baking.
  • Storage: Store leftovers covered at room temperature for up to 2 days, or refrigerate for up to 5 days. Reheat briefly in the microwave.
  • Cream cheese glaze: For a richer topping, beat 4 oz softened cream cheese with 1 cup powdered sugar and 2 tablespoons lemon juice.
